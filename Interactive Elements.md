# Interactive Elements

### Game 1: Truth or Dare (The Public Spectacle)

Because this game relies on public performance, it transitions to a flat screen perfectly. The edge-aligned windows ensure everyone can read the prompts simultaneously without twisting their necks.

**Phase 1: The Spin**

- **Trigger:** A guest opens their edge window and taps "Truth or Dare."
- **Center Canvas:** The ambient table lighting dims. A massive, glowing arrow appears in the exact center and spins rapidly. It slowly decelerates and locks onto one physical drink on the table.
- **Edge Windows:** All four windows display: *"The Arrow has chosen [Drink Name]!"*

**Phase 2: The Hot Seat**

- **Center Canvas:** A tight, pulsing spotlight locks directly underneath the chosen victim's glass.
- **Edge Windows:** The text on all four sides updates to ask the victim a question, displaying two massive buttons underneath: **[ TRUTH ]** or **[ DARE ]**.
- *Interaction:* Even though the buttons are visible to everyone, social pressure dictates that only the spotlighted victim reaches out to tap their choice.

**Phase 3: The Global Broadcast**

- **Trigger:** The victim taps "Dare."
- **Center Canvas:** The entire table flashes with a deep red ripple to visually announce the choice to the rest of the room.
- **Edge Windows:** A random dare is pulled from the database. The exact text (e.g., *"Dare: Let the person to your left take a sip of your drink"*) is rendered right-side up on all four edges of the table. The entire group reads it and reacts at the exact same time.

### Game 2: King's Game / 國王遊戲 (The Blind Dealer Adaptation)

The classic King's Game requires the King to issue commands to secret numbers (e.g., "Number 2 kisses Number 4") without knowing who holds those numbers. Since we cannot hide numbers on a flat screen, **the table itself becomes the blind dealer.** The King chooses the *action*, and the table randomly chooses the *victims*.

**Phase 1: Crowning the King**

- **Trigger:** A guest selects "King's Game."
- **Center Canvas:** A chaotic web of light connects all the drinks on the table. It flashes rapidly before snapping to a single glass. A brilliant, glowing crown aura permanently surrounds that physical coaster. Everyone immediately knows who is in charge.

**Phase 2: The King's Menu**

- **Edge Windows:** The windows update. For the regular guests, it simply says: *"Bow to the King. Waiting for a decree..."* * **The King's Interaction:** The King's edge window displays a list of Actions/Rules to choose from (e.g., *Action: Swap Drinks*, *Action: Answer a Truth*, *Action: Down your glass*). The King taps one to lock it in, and then hits a giant **[ EXECUTE DECREE ]** button.

**Phase 3: The Roulette Spotlight (The Blind Selection)**

- **Center Canvas:** This is where the suspense happens. The King has chosen the punishment, but no one knows who will receive it. Fast-moving spotlights begin frantically darting between all the *other* drinks on the table (excluding the King's).
- **Edge Windows:** All windows flash: *"The King commands a Drink Swap! Choosing targets..."*

**Phase 4: The Decree Revealed**

- **Center Canvas:** The darting lights suddenly freeze, locking bright halos under two specific, randomly chosen glasses.
- **Edge Windows:** The text locks in on all four sides: *"The King has spoken! The [Apple Tart] and the [Momo Sour] must swap drinks!"*
